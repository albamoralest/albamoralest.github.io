Alba Morales T.

BSc Computer Science, MSc Infomation Security and Audit.

Alba is a Research Associate in the Knowledge Media Institute (KMi) at the Open University. She is part of the ISDS (Intelligent Systems and Data Science) research group in KMi focused on the use of semantic technologies to manage large amounts of data.

Research

Alba's PhD work was focused on the secure and private exchange of personal data in the Smart City environment. Specifically, the use and exchange of healthcare data to support emergency services during a fire building evacuation. Her work's first results propose a methodology to identify and extract useful information from health records to support emergency services with a privacy-aware method. She is currently studying the design of Privacy-Aware Intelligent Systems; the objective is to use a knowledge engineering approach and a common-sense knowledge base to identify sensitive data, extract relevant information, and process it to answer task-oriented questions while avoiding data disclosure.

Alba also shares an interest in areas related to cybersecurity and data privacy.

Background

Alba has a bachelor in Computer Science from the University of Cuenca, Ecuador and has a master degree in Information Security and Audit from the University of Greenwich in the UK.

Alba has professional experience working in the private and public sector since 2005 and developed experience working as software developer and project manager. Before starting her PhD, she worked as Information Security Specialist for several years, where she specialised in auditing Industrial Control Systems. She has experience implementing measures and processes to comply with national security norms and international electricity industry standards.
